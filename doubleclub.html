Project Overview

The Prada Double Club Miami takes the cultural climate of two powerful human backdrops to embody a refreshing take on a universal experience. Showcasing the strong Caribbean and Western atmospheres that co-exist in Miami, artist Carsten Höller transcends art into a real and interactive nightclub. By juxtaposing two authentic and uncompromising environments with a permeable boundary, an unexplored third form arises.

Create an exciting, inticing website for a pop-up nightclub, which had a theme that needed a little explaining.

Research

The design process began with finding and gathering information about the club, and since we found that it was a pop-up club that had been in other international cities as well, I thought we should do research into the city of Miami so we could differentiate it from other cities. As well as gathering inspiration from other websites and artists. We then came together and discussed what we thought were the most interesting and useful points about what we found and established some moodboards. I suggested that we create separate moodboards so that we would have more than one line of investigation to work on. Through our research, we found that the artist really wanted to present a lot of opposing ideas, so in our designs, we wanted to use a lot of duplication. We also did brainstorming about what would entice a person living in Miami to come to the Double Club. Miami is a city famous for its electric nightlife, so we wanted to provide a feel from our first points of contact that would make them think this is not your typical club.

Graphic Assets

Using our moodboards we began to come up with a visual direction that would attract Miamians to the club, and work well in the digital space. Each one of us took a moodboard and used our user brainstorming in an attempt to create graphical assets. We wanted to make sure we used the neon, saturated colours that Miami was often associated with, but also be sure to juxtapose it with simple design elements like lines and sans-serif fonts, just like the whole idea behind the Double Club. Our group worked very well as a team and was very open to collaboration, if we ever saw what someone else was doing and liked it, we encouraged each other to make a copy and experiment with the design.

COMMENT

Poster Ideation

Final Posters

We landed on using these posters which I had created. They had a strong focus on typography and evoked a feeling of something hiding behind the type. The alternating direction of the type gives a rhythm to the poster. While the transparent type and ripped paper gives a unique texture and creates interesting figure-ground relationships between the image and type. When designing this poster, I wanted to viewers get a sneak peak of the monochromatic and polychromatic halves of the club. So I decided to “hide” the vibrant Miami colours behind a typical type-forward poster.

Micro-site

With our finalized posters, we moved to using them as a baseline for our microsite design process. We tried implementing as much of the poster’s visual design direction into the microsite so they feel unified. The goal of the microsite is to introduce the users to a uniquely fascinating clubbing experience and entice them into buying a ticket for entrance to the club.

Final Designs

Personalized Experience Flow

Our personalized shopping experience was designed to emulate the service you would get in Burb stores. When designing this feature I did research into what users may want out of their cannabis experience. Like whether they are using it to relax or re-energize, how they want to consume their cannabis, and what kind of feeling they want out of it. After considering all these factors, the feature would provide suggestions based on what the user wants.
